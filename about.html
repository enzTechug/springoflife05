Our Story & Mission

Restoring dignity, hope, and care to the elderly in Uganda.

Our Story

Spring of Life began with a simple calling: to bring dignity, care, and companionship to elderly men and women who had been forgotten. After witnessing the daily struggles of vulnerable elders in Zombo District—loneliness, hunger, and little access to medical support—we felt moved to act.

What started as a small personal effort has grown into a community initiative that provides food support, home visits, health assistance, and opportunities for elders to stay active through traditional Alur handcrafts. Spring of Life continues to serve with compassion, transparency, and the belief that every elderly person deserves to be seen, valued, and cared for.

Our Mission

Our mission is to support vulnerable elderly people in Zombo District with food, home visits, healthcare assistance, and meaningful community engagement—restoring dignity, hope, and love to their lives.

About the Founder

Spring of Life Elderly Care was founded by Nancy Viback, a compassionate individual based in Sweden, whose heart remains deeply connected to the elderly in Zombo District, Uganda.

Guided by faith and love, Nancy envisioned a project that would bring care, dignity, and hope to those often forgotten. Though choosing to remain private, the founder continues to support and guide the mission from afar, working closely with local volunteers and partners to ensure the elderly are cared for with compassion and respect.

The founder's belief is simple — that every elderly person deserves to live with dignity, love, and peace, and that through collective care, lives can be restored and hope renewed.

What We Do

Food & Nutrition

Weekly nutritious meals and warm blankets for vulnerable elders.

Home Visits & Care

Regular visits offering companionship and basic care support.

Healthcare Support

Medical care, clinic referrals, and essential medications.
